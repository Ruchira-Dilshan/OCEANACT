Detailed Content on Sustainable Fishing

Why Sustainable Fishing Matters

Fishing supports more than 3.3 billion people in the world and livelihood to approximately 600 million people, primarily in the developing world. This has to be done without depleting the same stocks that have to be replenished. communities depend upon. Goal 14.4 More precisely, it requires the goal of ending overfishing and the use of. science-based management plans by 2020 - a deadline which the world has missed largely.

The Overfishing Crisis

The FAO has estimated that 34.2 percent of the global monitored stocks of fish are overfished or in other words, they are being overfished. grown more rapidly than they multiply. The rest 60 percent are fished to their maximum sustainable capacity. Only underfished stocks about 6% of stocks. This virtually has no safety margin against error, climatic shocks, or ecological. change.

It is mainly caused by industrial fishing fleets that are usually subsidised by the governments. Many operate in the waters of developing countries under access schemes that bring very little benefit to the local communities as well as catching gigantic numbers of fish.

Sustainable Fishing Methods

  • Pole and line fishing — Targets individual fish, eliminating bycatch and habitat damage.
  • Fish aggregating device (FAD)-free tuna — Avoids the indiscriminate catch of juvenile fish and non-target species.
  • Aquaculture (well-managed) — When properly regulated, farmed shellfish and seaweed can actually improve water quality.
  • Seasonal closures — Allowing fish populations to recover by prohibiting fishing during breeding seasons.

Making Better Consumer Choices

Each seafood purchase will be in favor or against a sustainable ocean. Find the MSC (Marine Stewardship. The label on packaging of Council) blue fish or the label on packaging of the Aquaculture Stewardship Council. Apps like the Good Fish Guide (a UK-based program by the Marine Conservation Society) assists shoppers with making a responsible choice.

Introducing the question to restaurants and fish and chip shops on where the seafood was obtained also sends a strong market. signal — and more businesses are getting responsive to consumer demand, transparency.
